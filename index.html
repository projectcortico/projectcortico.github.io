Minimally invasive procedures and complex biopsies like brain tumour excisions, liver, lung and renal biopsies and ablations require accurate intra-operative image guidance and visualization to avoid complications. Surgical navigation systems represent the state-of-art in the current global standard of care.

Current solutions have problems that span across product design, inherent limitations of the technology and complex workflows. The cost of the tracking technology and the economy of scale that the large companies in the navigation business have to deal with create a high total cost of ownership for these systems. All these factors have limited the market potential and widespread adoption of navigation system. In India, the market penetration is very low. We plan to build an integrated visualization and navigation system which provides:

  • Essential preoperative and intraoperative data access without disrupting surgery workflow
  • A gesture based natural user interface for ease of registration and visual feedback on its accuracy
  • Proprietary technology for head and tool tracking
The system has potential to make future minimally invasive surgeries quicker, more reliable and effective
